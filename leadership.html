CEO - Dorothy Qedani Mahlangu

Qedani Mahlangu is a Businesswoman, Farmer, Public Sector Finance and Governance Specialist and Social activist with leadership expertise and knowledge in government, corporate and farming. Her qualifications include:

  • MSc Finance (Economic Policy) - University of London (SOAS)
  • B Comm Honours (Economics) - University of Western Cape
  • Advanced Diploma in Economics - University of Western Cape
  • Diploma (Primary Education) - Westminster College of Education
  • Diploma (Property Development and Investment) - University of Cape Town

Her journey within social activism began in high school, where she was a student activist at her high school Mzinoni High. There she recruited and participated in the underground structures of the African National Congress (ANC).

Years later her journey with the public sector began, where she was elected to be part of the Branch Executive Committee (BEC) and serve the Youth League in different capacities and at different levels (East Rand and known as Ekurhuleni Zone, then called (Regional Executive Committee (REC) and post 1994 called the Provincial Executive Committee (PEC).

The ANC Youth League National Executive (NEC) then elected her to serve in the National Working Committee (NWC) and be responsible for Gender Issues, having recognized her astounding leadership qualities and expertise. In her role she was instrumental in developing the sexual harassment code, which became approved and passed. She then served in a multitude of key leadership roles within the local Gauteng Government and boarder Government, which included:

  • Member of the Gauteng Legislature: Public Accounts and Finance Committee
  • Gauteng Whip in the NCOP
  • Trustee and Member of Management Committee, Political Office Bearers Pension Fund
  • Chairperson of the Select Committee on Finance: oversaw the Minister of Finance, South African Reserve Bank (SARB), South African Revenue Services (SARS), Financial Services Board (FSB), Statistics SA amongst others
  • Member of the Audit Commission: oversight and policy approvals on the Auditor General’s office
  • Member of the Gautrain Political Committee: oversaw the funding and operations of the project)
  • MEC for Local Government
  • MEC for Health and Social Development
  • MEC for Economic Development
  • MEC for Infrastructure Development (Public Works)
  • MEC Health: Awarded Best Province, Department of Health, 2016
  • Gauteng Provincial Legislature (MPL): Awarded knighthood (Cavalier) from the President of Italy Mr. Giorgio Napolitano in recognition, inter alia, for her excellent work performed with the Italian community in South Africa

Her knowledge, expertise and the partnerships she had formed across the multiple sectors of social development, health, economic development, governance and finance equipped and enabled her to venture into her lifelong passion of farming, leading her to start her farming business Malilwane in 2020.

Since running Malilwane, with the primary focus on vegetable produce and crops such as Butternuts, Pumpkins, Hemp & Chilies, she has been able to distribute locally and export to Europe and The USA. She has also been recently contracted to grow other crops (e.g. yellow maize, tomatoes peppers) and plans to continue growing, building and sustaining the farming community in which she operates from both an impact and longevity perspective financially, educationally and environmentally as she competes on both a local and global scale, creating and cultivating the next generation of future female and young farmers in underdeveloped areas.
